A Little More About Me

Born and raised in New Orleans, I am no stranger to celebrations. From an early age, I can remember there always being something or someone we were throwing a party for. It was only suiting that I gravitated towards the event industry professionally. I've gotten to plan, coordinate, create, and attend some of the most unique experiences of my life. You never do the same event twice, you're always improving, enhancing, growing from the last event. While I loved everything about events, I started to realize it wasn't something I wanted to do as a career for the rest of my life. After seven years of planning and assisting at bridal shows, weddings, bar and bat mitzvahs, baby showers, and more, I felt like I needed a change. I also happened to come across a program called Skillcrush around the same time.

Skillcrush is an interactive online learning community that offers Blueprints teaching digital skills and demystifying technology. I felt like my passion had been reignited and I was hungry to learn more. Before Skillcrush, my extent of computer skills were a beginner web development class in college and had a pretty sweet MySpace page back in the day, but hadnâ€™t really touched real code ever before.

As I began to consider how to adjust my career, I was interested in how I could further this new skill of web development. With the confidence I had gained from learning a little bit of code on my own, I decided to apply for Nashville Software School's full-time, immersive web development bootcamp. The past few months at NSS have been some of the hardest and most rewarding I've experienced. We've built some awesome things already and I can't wait to continue to build and grow in this new venture.
